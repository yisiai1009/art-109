Artist Statement

Mood Buttons turns emotion into a shareable state. A single emoji click changes the color, text, and link of the page. The mood lives inside the URL, making the browser itself part of the artwork. Offline, the core mechanic vanishes.

The project sketches how online emotions travel through compact signalsâ€”reactions, stickers, tiny icons. A mood becomes a portable address. Sharing that address carries tone forward: someone else opens the link and lands on a page already colored by your feeling.

Visual style is simple and direct: large emoji buttons, color fields, instant feedback, and brief moments of spectacle such as full-screen emoji rain. No feeds, accounts, or tracking; everything runs locally in the browser. Itâ€™s a small, playful use of web-native materials.
